A Novel Framework for Artificial Intelligence in Legal Discovery

Jane Doe1 and John Smith2

1Department of Computer Science, University of Technology
2Faculty of Law, State University

Abstract

This paper presents a comprehensive framework for the integration of large language models (LLMs) into the electronic discovery (e-discovery) process. We propose a pipeline that significantly reduces manual review time while maintaining high precision and recall in document categorization. Our methodology introduces a novel prompting strategy designed specifically for complex legal terminology and ambiguous contexts.

1. Introduction

The sheer volume of digital information generated in modern corporate environments presents a significant challenge during legal proceedings. Traditional predictive coding and technology-assisted review (TAR) protocols often struggle with nuanced language and context shifts. Recent advancements in transformer-based architectures offer a promising alternative.

As noted by previous research [1], the bottleneck in legal document review is rarely data extraction, but rather semantic interpretation. We aim to address this by formalizing the task as a few-shot learning problem bounded by specific legal constraints.
